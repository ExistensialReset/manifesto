# PHASE-BY-PHASE STRUCTURAL ADJUSTMENT

---

## PHASE 0: SOLO EXPLORATION

**Original Realistic:** 2–4 months  
**Adjusted Realistic:** 4–8 months  

### Why:

- Compatible humans are rare.
- Philosophical alignment ≠ behavioral alignment.
- Most people require time to shift from curiosity to commitment.

**Drop-off expectation:** 50% of initial interest does not convert to Micro-Circle formation.

---

## PHASE 1: MICRO-CIRCLE

**Original Realistic:** 8–12 months to readiness for Baseline  
**Adjusted Realistic:** 18–36 months  

### Structural Frictions:

1. First conflict resolution success does not predict long-term resilience.
2. Second or third conflict often causes member loss.
3. Economic exhaustion reduces engagement.
4. Life events (illness, relocation, job change) destabilize continuity.

### Historical Pattern:
Most small intentional groups dissolve between years 1–3.

To reach Baseline readiness, Micro-Circles must survive:

- At least 2–3 major stress events
- 1–2 member exits without collapse
- Unequal resource tension

That alone extends timeline significantly.

---

## PHASE 2: BASELINE CIRCLE

**Original Realistic:** 16–20 months  
**Adjusted Realistic:** 36–60 months  

### Why the Expansion:

Infrastructure friction is consistently underestimated.

Common real-world barriers:

- Land access or lease stability
- Zoning constraints
- Insurance requirements
- Liability exposure
- Financial compliance
- Accessibility requirements
- Energy setup costs
- Governance fatigue

Additionally:

20–30 people coordinating regularly requires:

- Administrative load
- Emotional mediation labor
- Rotational discipline (rare in volunteer systems)

**Burnout probability:** High if leadership rotation is imperfect.

---

## PHASE 3: FLOW NODE

**Original Realistic:** 25–30 months  
**Adjusted Realistic:** 5–10 years total from Baseline stage**

### Major Bottlenecks:

1. Production infrastructure capital cost
2. Professional licensing & liability
3. Energy reliability
4. Housing density management
5. Cultural fragmentation at scale

Professionals (nurses, educators, engineers) experience:

- Elevated burnout in communal systems
- Income equality tensions
- External credential constraints

Nodes historically take multiple years just to reach stable food production and governance coherence.

---

## PHASE 4: REGIONAL NETWORK

**Original Realistic:** 31–42 months  
**Adjusted Realistic:** 8–15 years after first stable Node**

Inter-node complexity increases exponentially:

- Cultural variance
- Resource asymmetry
- Travel overhead
- Communication fatigue
- Governance creep

Most federated cooperative systems plateau regionally for decades before stabilizing.

---

## PHASE 5: GLOBAL NETWORK

**Original Realistic:** 30–60 years  
**Adjusted Realistic:** 50–100+ years**

Global coordination requires:

- Generational trust transfer
- Legal normalization
- Cultural adaptation
- Infrastructure layering
- Resistance from dominant economic paradigms

No alternative system in modern history has globally normalized faster than generational scale.
