# RESOURCE TRACKING & COORDINATION IN FLOW

---

## Introduction

Without currency, tracking resources becomes essential. Flow ensures that resources are distributed fairly and efficiently while maintaining **freedom, privacy, and minimal bureaucracy**.

---

## 1. Local Node Coordination

- Direct resource flow to residents
- Automatic tracking via lightweight digital ledger
- Transparency without intrusion
- Node components: distribution hub, Lyceum workshop, healthcare team, production coordination

---

## 2. Regional Coordination

- Nodes exchange surpluses and deficits
- Shared specialization for efficiency
- Coordination node oversees flows with human oversight
- Still no money: only resource quantities tracked

---

## 3. Global Coordination

- 5% of resources requiring global exchange: coffee, tea, spices, rare minerals, specialized machinery
- Global Flow Network logs production, shipments, and expected arrivals
- Reciprocity principle ensures balance
- Blockchain-style ledger for traceability
- Minimal interference with local Nodes

---

## 4. Data Structures & Systems

- Digital ledger: resource type, quantity, origin, destination, timestamp
- Node dashboard: visual overview, surplus alerts, coordination suggestions
- Coordination algorithms: balance flows, consider seasonal data, population needs, transport capacity
- Humans make final decisions in edge cases

---

## 5. Ensuring Fairness Without Money

- Baseline guarantees essentials
- Contributions recognized socially
- Transparency + accountability through audit by Node members
- Soft social pressure encourages participation
- Peer review ensures professional quality

---

## 6. Security & Privacy

- No personal activity tracking
- Resource logs anonymous unless safety/healthcare requires
- End-to-end encryption for Node data
- Access controlled by community and professionals

---

## 7. System Benefits

- No currency dependency
- Equitable distribution
- Encourages engagement and contribution
- Maintains trust without surveillance
- Scalable from local to global
