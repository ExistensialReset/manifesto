## Purpose

The Lyceum & Musaeum are nodes for **learning, creation, and professional development**.  
They connect people with tools, workshops, seminars, and knowledge resources.

---

## Core Principles

1. **Free Access**  
   - Anyone in the Node can access workshops, labs, and libraries
   - No fees, no applications

2. **Voluntary Participation**  
   - Attend seminars, classes, or labs by choice
   - Optional remote or digital attendance, with sign-language interpretation when needed

3. **Integration With Flow**  
   - Lyceum coordinates with healthcare, local production, and professional teams
   - Knowledge is open-source and shared across Nodes

---

## Functions

- Workshops for practical skills: carpentry, textiles, electronics
- Labs for scientific experiments and research
- Seminars and lectures on topics from medicine to philosophy
- Collaborative projects between residents, professionals, and researchers
- Community arts, music, and cultural activities

---

## Professional & Educational Integration

- Mentorship programs connect volunteers and professionals
- Training ensures that standards are maintained across critical fields:
  - Medicine
  - Therapy and counseling
  - Technical professions
- Residents can pursue learning or mastery without financial barriers

---

## Key Features

- Digital scheduling system for workshops, labs, and home visits
- Fully accessible for all functional abilities
- Local fabrication tools and resources available
- Peer recognition and social credit for contribution and master
