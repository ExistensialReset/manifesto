## 2. SOCIAL RECOGNITION SCORE (SRS): The Access Protocol

**Definition:** The Social Recognition Score (SRS) is a dynamic, non-fungible metric that quantifies an individual's verifiable contribution to the collective Flow. SRS cannot be used for baseline needs (guaranteed by the CR) but is the sole logistical key granting access to complex resources and influence.

### SRS Mechanism (The Anti-Mammon Protocol)

* **Non-Speculative:** SRS is earned, never purchased, traded, or leveraged against debt. It is tied only to the verified utility of the Flow contribution.
* **Decay Function:** SRS is subject to a controlled, slow decay rate over time (e.g., 5-10% annually). This function discourages resting on past laurels and enforces the principle of **continuous, active contribution** to maintain access to advanced resources.
* **Zero-Sum-Immunity:** The system ensures that the earning of SRS by one individual does not logically diminish the potential for another individual to earn SRS. The total potential SRS is tied to the total capacity for Flow contribution within the system.
* **Allocation:** SRS is primarily allocated through **Peer Review Consensus** or **Verifiable Protocol Metrics** (e.g., bug fixes, open-source adoption, scientific validation).

### Access Rights

SRS is the only logistical key required for access to:
* Advanced research tools and facilities.
* Specialized education tracks and mentorship.
* Unique travel rights (e.g., long-distance, complex logistics).
* Positions of significant influence within the Existential Sovereign governance structure.
