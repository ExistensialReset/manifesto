## 2. SOCIAL RECOGNITION SCORE (SRS): The Access Protocol

**Definition:** The Social Recognition Score (SRS) is a dynamic, non-fungible metric that quantifies an individual's verifiable contribution to the collective Flow. SRS cannot be used for baseline needs (guaranteed by the CR) but grants access to complex resources.

**Mechanism (Non-Speculative):**
1. **SRS is earned, never purchased.** It cannot be speculated upon, leveraged against debt, and it slowly decays over time to encourage continuous contribution to Flow.
2. **Access Rights:** SRS is the sole logistical key required to access complex, rare, or advanced resources and privileges (e.g., advanced research tools, specialized education, unique travel rights, high-influence positions within governance).
3. **Public Record:** All SRS allocations and contributions are visible under **Lex Dualis Veritas**.
4. **Purpose:** The system is engineered to reward advanced, meaningful contribution, ensuring that the highest levels of resources are allocated by merit (verified contribution to Flow), not by capital accumulation (Mammon).
