## Ⅱ. THE BIOLOGICAL ENGINE
We utilize **Genetically Modified Yeast** (*Saccharomyces cerevisiae*) containing the human insulin gene sequence.

### 1. Transformation (The "Backpack" Setup)
- **Action:** Introduce the insulin-producing DNA plasmid into the yeast cells using heat-shock (42°C for 30 seconds).
- **Selection:** Grow the yeast on an antibiotic-laced medium. Only the yeast that successfully took up the DNA (which also contains resistance) will survive. This ensures 100% of your colony is "working."

### 2. Fermentation (The Bioreactor)
- **Environment:** Sterile sugar-water (glucose), nitrogen source, and trace minerals.
- **Hardware:** A modified pressure cooker acting as a bioreactor.
- **Control:** Temperature must be maintained at 30-36°C. Continuous aeration (filtered air) and gentle agitation are mandatory to prevent cell death.
- **Safety:** The air intake must pass through a 0.22-micron HEPA filter to prevent airborne contaminants from entering the "pool."

### 3. Separation (The Harvest)
- **Action:** Once the yeast has produced a high concentration of pro-insulin into the growth medium, the liquid is centrifuged.
- **Goal:** To separate the living cells (sediment) from the liquid containing the insulin (supernatant).

### 4. Purification (The Smart Sieve)
- **Action:** Use **Ion-Exchange Chromatography**. Pass the supernatant through a resin column.
- **Logic:** The insulin protein binds to the resin beads while contaminants flow through.
- **Elution:** Wash the column with a buffer (pH-adjusted solution) to release the pure insulin.

## Ⅲ. VALIDATION PROTOCOL
Before use, the batch must pass three tests:
1. **Purity Test:** High-Performance Liquid Chromatography (HPLC) to ensure no yeast proteins remain.
2. **Concentration Test:** Measuring the International Units (IU) per ml to prevent dosing errors.
3. **Sterility Test:** Ensuring no live bacteria are present in the final vial.
