## Ⅱ. TECHNICAL SPECIFICATION (English)

### 1. The Growth Medium (For 1 Liter)
To force *Penicillium chrysogenum* to synthesize penicillin, a specific mineral and sugar balance is required.

**Reagents:**
- **Lactose (Milk Sugar):** 44.0 g (The primary carbon source)
- **Corn Steep Liquor:** 35.0 g (Nitrogen source. Can be replaced by potato starch/extract in emergencies)
- **Potassium Dihydrogen Phosphate ($KH_2PO_4$):** 4.0 g (Buffer)
- **Magnesium Sulfate ($MgSO_4 \cdot 7H_2O$):** 0.25 g (Metabolic catalyst)
- **Sodium Nitrate ($NaNO_3$):** 3.0 g (Secondary nitrogen)
- **Zinc Sulfate ($ZnSO_4 \cdot 7H_2O$):** 0.044 g (Trace element)
- **Copper Sulfate ($CuSO_4 \cdot 5H_2O$):** 0.005 g (Trace element)
- **Distilled Water:** Up to 1.0 Liter

### 2. Procedure:
1. **Preparation:** Dissolve all minerals and sugars in distilled water using the **Magnetic Stirrer**. Ensure the pH is adjusted to 6.0–6.5.
2. **Sterilization:** Pour the medium into wide-mouthed jars (2cm depth). Sterilize in the **Pressure Cooker** at 121°C for 20 minutes.
3. **Inoculation:** In the **Glovebox**, add *Penicillium* spores to the cooled jars.
4. **Incubation:** Store in the dark at 23–25°C for 7–10 days. A mold mat will form on the surface.
5. **Extraction:** Filter the golden-yellow liquid (supernatant). Adjust pH to 2.5 using phosphoric acid and chill to 0°C. Extract into an organic solvent (e.g., ethyl acetate), then back-extract into a potassium acetate buffer to create stable Penicillin K salts.
