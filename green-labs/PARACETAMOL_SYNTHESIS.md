## Ⅰ. WARNING: CHEMICAL PURITY & LIVER SAFETY
Paracetamol (Acetaminophen) is only safe when 100% pure. Impure synthesis can leave residues of **p-aminophenol**, which is toxic. **Validation through Melting Point analysis (target: 169°C) is mandatory** before human use. Do not skip the recrystallization step.

## Ⅱ. TECHNICAL SPECIFICATION (English)

### 1. The Chemical Reaction
The synthesis is an acetylation of **p-aminophenol** using **acetic anhydride**.

### 2. Required Reagents:
- **p-aminophenol (4.0 g)**
- **Acetic anhydride (5.0 ml)**
- **Distilled Water (15 ml)**
- **Sodium Acetate (optional buffer)**

### 3. Procedure:
1. **Dissolution:** In a 50ml flask, suspend 4.0g of p-aminophenol in 15ml of distilled water.
2. **Acetylation:** While stirring constantly using the **Magnetic Stirrer (Elinor’s Design)**, slowly add 5.0ml of acetic anhydride. The reaction will generate heat.
3. **Heating:** Gently heat the mixture to approx. 60°C for 10 minutes to ensure the reaction is complete. All solids should dissolve.
4. **Crystallization:** Remove from heat and let it cool to room temperature, then place in an **ice bath**. Crude paracetamol crystals will precipitate.
5. **Vacuum Filtration:** Filter the crystals and wash with a small amount of ice-cold distilled water.
6. **Recrystallization (Critical):** Dissolve the crude crystals in the minimum amount of boiling water. Let it cool very slowly. This forces the paracetamol to form pure crystals while pushing impurities into the liquid.
7. **Drying:** Dry the final pure crystals in a sterile environment (SAB/Glovebox).
