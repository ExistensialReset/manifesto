## 1. Introduction

Sigma Phenomenology (Σ-Ph) is the observation framework for **how knowledge emerges, propagates, and decays** within complex socio-technical systems.  
It differs from classical phenomenology by focusing not on individual consciousness, but **systemic consciousness and flows**.

> "Σ-Ph is not about what people think; it is about what a system ‘knows’ and how it knows it."

---

## 2. Core Principles

1. **Knowledge as Flow** – Information is not static; its value is a function of velocity, redundancy, and reach.  
2. **Embodied System Awareness** – Systems “feel” constraints via resource feedback, errors, and environmental signals.  
3. **Cross-Scale Coupling** – Micro-decisions propagate to macro-effects and vice versa.  
4. **Phenomenal Collapse Points** – Moments when knowledge becomes unusable or misinterpreted due to entropy or misalignment.

---

## 3. Measurement Proxies

### 3.1 Flow Integrity

| Proxy | Measurement | Example |
|-------|------------|---------|
| **Throughput Consistency** | Variance in information flow | High variance = instability in knowledge |
| **Redundancy vs. Fragility** | Number of independent nodes with same info | Single source → fragile |
| **Feedback Latency** | Time between decision and observable effect | Delays reduce system learning |

---

### 3.2 Entropy Management

| Proxy | Measurement | Example |
|-------|------------|---------|
| **Noise-to-Signal Ratio** | % of errors in reports | Too high → collapse of usable knowledge |
| **Knowledge Half-Life** | Time until a protocol or decision loses relevance | Short half-life = high volatility |
| **Archival Efficacy** | % of stored knowledge retrieved successfully | Failure → systemic memory loss |

---

### 3.3 Attention Economy

| Proxy | Measurement | Example |
|-------|------------|---------|
| **Cognitive Load Distribution** | % nodes overloaded vs. idle | Balanced → better systemic cognition |
| **Decision Saturation** | Number of decisions per node per unit time | Avoid burnout, collapse |
| **Focal Drift** | % deviation from aligned goals | Measured via monitoring dashboards |

---

## 4. Emergent Behaviors

- **Resonance:** Knowledge flows amplify each other, producing innovation cascades  
- **Damping:** Overcontrol or hierarchy reduces systemic learning  
- **Echo Chambers:** Feedback loops trapped in limited perspective  
- **Sigma Collapse:** Knowledge becomes inert; system fails to act adaptively

---

## 5. Practical Implementation

1. Map knowledge nodes (people, tools, processes)  
2. Identify critical flows and collapse points  
3. Measure flow integrity and entropy metrics  
4. Identify interventions (training, process redesign, redundancy)  
5. Monitor systemic cognition over time

---

## 6. Limitations

- Metrics are **contextual**, cannot fully capture human judgment  
- Requires **continuous observation**, not snapshots  
- Cross-cultural systems may require **proxy recalibration**

---

## 7. Critical Reflection

Σ-Ph gives a **dynamic lens** on organizational wisdom, but the danger is over-reliance on metrics: the system may “look” intelligent without actually being wise.  
Integration with EV (Eternal Wisdom) scoring ensures that **systemic cognition serves life-aligned purposes**.
