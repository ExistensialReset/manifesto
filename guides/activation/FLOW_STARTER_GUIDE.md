### PART I: THE FIRST 7 DAYS (SOLO)

**DAY 1 ‚Äì Monday: See Your Mammon-Grip (ALT: Track Safety)** *Standard:* Carry a notebook. Every time you feel fear today ‚Äì write it down. (Fear of being late, bills, etc.)  
*Trauma-Sensitive:* Track moments of relative safety instead. (10:30 ‚Äì Door locked, felt safe.)  
*Evening reflection:* Count them. That's your data.

**DAY 2 ‚Äì Tuesday: Find One (1) Minute of Calm (L)** Find 60 seconds where you are not afraid. (Hands in warm water, cat purring, noticing breath.)  
*Evening reflection:* "I found calm at [time]." You just proved to your body: fear is not constant.

**DAY 3 ‚Äì Wednesday: Do Something Pointless (S)** Do something that serves no purpose. Draw a circle, hum, walk the long way.  
*Evening reflection:* "I wasted [X] minutes on nothing." You just proved: life is not only productivity.

**DAY 4 ‚Äì Thursday: See One Human (I)** Actually see someone today. Not transactionally. Just for one second feel that: They exist, you exist, you exist together.  
*Evening reflection:* "I saw [name]. And they saw me." You just proved: you are not alone.

**DAY 5 ‚Äì Friday: Compare Your Data** Look at Monday's notes. Has your count changed? No judgment. Just noticing.  
*Insight:* If fear-count increased ‚Äì that's often increased awareness, not increased fear.

**DAY 6 ‚Äì Saturday: Give Without Getting Back** Give time, words, or food without expecting anything back. Walk away. No thanks needed.  
*Evening reflection:* "I gave [X]. And I walked away." This is Baseline-thinking: no one is indebted.

**DAY 7 ‚Äì Sunday: Ask Yourself One Question** Sit quietly for 10 minutes. Ask: "If I was not afraid ‚Äì what would I do?" Let your *body* answer.  
*Evening reflection:* Save this answer. It's important.
