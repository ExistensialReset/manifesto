- People with mortgages, student debt, medical bills

## 2. THE SIX PHASES OF HYBRID SURVIVAL

### PHASE 0: AWARENESS (Months 1-3)
**State:** You work in Mammon but no longer believe in it  
**Goal:** Clear understanding of your current entanglement

#### Actions:
1. **Track Everything for 30 Days**
   - Every expense
   - Every hour worked
   - Every stress response
   - Every moment of joy

2. **Calculate True Baseline**
   - What do you *actually* need to survive?
   - Not what you're "supposed" to want—what keeps you alive and sane
   - Include:
     - Food (real cost, not restaurant/convenience)
     - Shelter (mortgage/rent + utilities)
     - Healthcare (premiums + out-of-pocket)
     - Transportation (to work, groceries, medical)
     - Internet (for work and connection)
     - Childcare (if applicable)

3. **Identify Parasitic Costs**
   - What are you paying for that serves Mammon, not you?
   - Examples:
     - Work clothes you hate
     - Car you only need for commute
     - Gym membership to counteract sedentary job
     - Therapy to cope with job stress
     - Alcohol/drugs to numb weekend anxiety

4. **Map Your Skills (Real Wealth)**
   - What can you do that creates actual value?
   - Cooking, repair, teaching, listening, organizing, growing food, coding, caregiving
   - **This is your Flow currency**

#### Output:
- One-page document: "My True Baseline" (€/$/SEK per month)
- List of 10+ skills you have that aren't monetized currently
- Emotional baseline: How do you feel most days? (This will improve)

---

### PHASE 1: REDUCE BLEEDING (Months 4-12)
**State:** Still employed full-time, but strategically cutting Mammon dependencies  
**Goal:** Lower True Baseline by 20-30% without sacrificing health

#### Actions:
1. **Eliminate One Parasitic Cost Every Month**
   - Month 4: Cancel subscription you don't use (streaming, gym, magazine)
   - Month 5: Reduce food waste by meal planning
   - Month 6: Carpool or bike to work (if safe)
   - Month 7: Buy second-hand instead of new (clothes, tools)
   - Month 8: Learn basic repair (YouTube is free)
   - Continue pattern...

2. **Build Micro-Resilience**
   - Start a windowsill herb garden (food sovereignty, tiny scale)
   - Learn to mend one piece of clothing
   - Fix one broken thing in your home yourself
   - Cook one meal entirely from scratch

3. **Financial Triage**
   - **Secured Debt (Mortgage/Car):** Keep paying—these are your shelter and mobility
   - **Unsecured Debt (Credit Cards):** Minimum payments only; prioritize food/shelter
   - **Student Loans:** If crushing, research income-based repayment or forbearance
   - **Emergency Fund:** If possible, save €500-1000 (3 months of this phase)

4. **Find Your First Circle Members (3-5 people)**
   - Not "recruits"—just people you trust
   - Share meals once/month
   - Practice gift economy (borrow tools, share skills)
   - No pressure—just connection

#### Output:
- True Baseline reduced by 20%
- €500+ emergency fund (if possible)
- 3-5 people you can call at 2am
- One skill learned (repair, cooking, growing)

---

### PHASE 2: PARALLEL INFRASTRUCTURE (Months 13-24)
**State:** Still employed, but building Flow systems alongside Mammon survival  
**Goal:** Create safety net that isn't employer-dependent

#### Actions:
1. **Resource Sharing with Circle**
   - Share tools (lawnmower, power drill, sewing machine)
   - Bulk-buy food together (cheaper per unit)
   - Organize skill swaps (I teach you coding, you teach me gardening)
   - Childcare co-op (if applicable)

2. **Pilot Baseline Projects**
   - **Food:** Community garden plot or balcony container garden
   - **Energy:** If you own home, research solar panels (long-term investment)
   - **Water:** Rainwater collection for garden (if legal)
   - **Repair:** Tool library or repair café in your area

3. **Reduce Work Hours (If Possible)**
   - Negotiate 4-day work week (even at reduced pay)
   - Go part-time (if healthcare isn't tied to full-time)
   - Freelance one day/week to test non-employer income

4. **Document Everything**
   - What works? What fails?
   - How much does Circle reduce costs?
   - How do you feel? (L × S × I check)

#### Output:
- Circle sustains 10-20% of your Baseline (food, childcare, repairs)
- Work hours reduced by 10-20% (if possible)
- Clear data on what Flow-infrastructure costs vs. saves

---

### PHASE 3: THE THRESHOLD TEST (Months 25-36)
**State:** You can imagine life without current job—but haven't jumped yet  
**Goal:** Verify that Circle + savings can sustain True Baseline for 6 months

#### The Calculation:
**Can you answer "yes" to all of these?**

1. **Food:** Can Circle + garden provide 50%+ of your calories?
2. **Shelter:** Can you pay rent/mortgage for 6 months without income?
3. **Healthcare:** Do you have:
   - Emergency fund for medical needs?
   - Or access to public healthcare?
   - Or Circle members with medical skills?
4. **Psychological:** Can you handle 6 months of uncertainty without breakdown?

**If ANY answer is "no"—stay in Phase 2 longer. This is not failure.**

#### Actions:
1. **Stress Test Your Systems**
   - Simulate job loss for 1 month (don't actually quit)
   - Live entirely on Circle + savings
   - Document stress, gaps, failures

2. **Plan Exit Strategy**
   - If you decide to leave job:
     - Give notice professionally
     - Don't burn bridges (you may need reference)
     - Negotiate severance if possible
   - If you decide to stay:
     - That's also valid
     - Continue Phase 2 indefinitely
     - Hybrid survival is legitimate long-term strategy

#### Output:
- 6-month financial cushion OR
- Circle proven to sustain 50%+ of Baseline OR
- Decision to remain hybrid (also success)

---

### PHASE 4: THE LEAP (Months 37+) [OPTIONAL]
**State:** You exit primary Mammon employment  
**Goal:** Survive first 6 months, then stabilize

#### If You Jump:

**Month 1-3: Detox**
- You will feel guilty for resting
- You will panic about money
- You will question everything
- **This is withdrawal from Mammon-programming**

**Protocols:**
- Daily check-in with Circle
- Physical activity (walks, gardening)
- Creative work (art, music, writing)
- No major decisions for 90 days

**Month 4-6: Reorientation**
- Calm (L) begins returning
- Spontaneity (S) emerges (you'll laugh more)
- Connection (I) deepens with Circle

**Month 7-12: Contribution Phase**
- You'll *want* to contribute (not forced)
- This is Flow activating
- Skills you offer naturally

**Month 13+: Stabilization**
- New normal established
- Baseline sustained by Circle + minimal external work
- You are now seed for others

#### Output:
- Survived 6 months outside primary employment
- Baseline maintained via Circle + flexible work
- Documented lessons for next person

---

### PHASE 5: HYBRID PERMANENCE [ALTERNATIVE PATH]
**State:** You choose to remain partially in Mammon indefinitely  
**Goal:** Optimize balance between security and sovereignty

#### Why This Is Valid:

- Chronic illness requiring employer healthcare
- Caring for family member who needs Mammon resources
- Enjoying your work (rare but possible)
- Temperamentally suited to hybrid approach

#### Actions:
1. **Maximize Flow Within Constraints**
   - Work 3 days/week for money
   - 4 days/week for Circle contributions
   - Maintain boundaries (no overtime, no guilt)

2. **Protect Your Calm**
   - Work is *tool*, not identity
   - Circle is *home*, not side project
   - Never let Mammon colonize your rest

3. **Document Hybrid Model**
   - This is legitimate path
   - Many will need this long-term
   - Your experience helps others

#### Output:
- Stable 30/70 split (30% Mammon, 70% Flow)
- No shame about partial participation
- Model for others who cannot fully exit

## 3. SPECIAL CASES

### 3.1 Single Parents

**Reality:** You cannot risk your children's stability on untested systems

**Hybrid Approach:**
- Phase 2 takes 3-5 years minimum
- Circle must include other parents (childcare co-op)
- School system participation likely necessary
- No judgment—your children's safety is Baseline

**Never apologize for going slowly.**

### 3.2 Chronic Illness / Disability

**Reality:** Healthcare in Mammon-world is often employer-tied

**Hybrid Approach:**
- Investigate public healthcare eligibility
- Build medical knowledge within Circle (but not as replacement for doctors)
- Emergency fund must be larger (medical costs)
- May need permanent hybrid status (this is fine)

**Your health is Baseline. Never compromise it for ideological purity.**

### 3.3 Caring for Elderly Parents

**Reality:** They may not understand Flow or be able to transition

**Hybrid Approach:**
- Their Baseline becomes part of yours
- You may need to maintain Mammon income for their care
- Circle can provide respite care
- This is high-I (Inkännande) work—it counts

**Caring for those who cannot transition is Flow, not failure.**

### 3.4 Debt That Won't Forgive

**Reality:** Some debt is mathematically impossible to repay

**Options:**
1. **Strategic Default**
   - If debt exceeds 50% of annual income and you own no assets
   - Consult bankruptcy lawyer (often free consultation)
   - Understand consequences (credit score, legal)
   - But sometimes bankruptcy is **debt abolition**

2. **Income-Based Repayment**
   - For student loans (if available in your country)
   - For medical debt (negotiate with hospital)
   - Minimum payments indefinitely

3. **Acknowledge Unforgivable Debt**
   - Some systems don't allow exit
   - This is structural violence, not personal failure
   - Do what you must to survive

**No moral judgment on how you handle impossible debt.**

## 4. PSYCHOLOGICAL SURVIVAL GUIDE

### 4.1 Guilt Management

**You will feel guilty for:**
- Resting
- Not optimizing every hour
- "Abandoning" career progression
- Not providing luxuries for children
- Taking time for yourself

**Protocols:**
1. **Name It:** "I am experiencing Mammon-guilt"
2. **Remember:** Guilt is programming, not reality
3. **Check Baseline:** Are basic needs met? Then you are succeeding
4. **Connect:** Talk to Circle about guilt—you'll find everyone has it

### 4.2 Panic Responses

**When panic hits (and it will):**

**Physical:**
- Breathing exercises (4-7-8: inhale 4, hold 7, exhale 8)
- Walk outside (even 5 minutes)
- Cold water on face (vagal nerve reset)

**Social:**
- Call Circle member (no explanation needed—just "I'm panicking")
- Share fear honestly ("I'm afraid I'll be homeless")
- Ask for grounding ("Tell me three true things")

**Cognitive:**
- Check Baseline (food, shelter, water—do you have them today?)
- If yes, you are safe right now
- Tomorrow is tomorrow's problem

### 4.3 Loneliness in Transition

**You will feel lonely because:**
- Friends still in Mammon won't understand
- Family may criticize your choices
- Circle is forming but not yet deep

**Protocols:**
- Document your journey (journal, blog, video)
- Connect with others attempting transition (online forums, local groups)
- Remember: Pioneers are always lonely at first
- Find one person who *gets it*—that's enough initially

## 5. WHEN TO ABORT TRANSITION

### 5.1 Valid Reasons to Return to Full Mammon

**Recognize when transition is harming you:**

1. **Medical Emergency Requiring Employer Insurance**
   - Cancer diagnosis
   - Mental health crisis needing intensive treatment
   - Child's serious illness

2. **Circle Collapse**
   - Key members leave
   - Conflict destroys trust
   - Infrastructure fails completely

3. **Psychological Breakdown**
   - Anxiety making you non-functional
   - Depression preventing basic self-care
   - Suicidal ideation

**If any of these occur: STOP. Return to stability. This is not failure.**

### 5.2 Dignified Return

**If you must return to Mammon employment:**

1. **No Shame**
   - You tried
   - You learned
   - Data is valuable even from "failure"

2. **Carry Flow Principles Forward**
   - Negotiate boundaries at new job
   - Maintain Circle connections
   - Protect your Calm (L)

3. **Document Lessons**
   - What worked?
   - What failed?
   - What would you do differently?
   - This helps next person

**Returning is not defeat—it is adaptation.**

## 6. METRICS FOR HYBRID SUCCESS

### 6.1 Not About Money

**Measure these instead:**

1. **Calm (L)**
   - Do you sleep better?
   - Are stress headaches reducing?
   - Can you enjoy moments without guilt?

2. **Spontaneity (S)**
   - Do you laugh more?
   - Have hobbies returned?
   - Do you create things for joy?

3. **Connection (I)**
   - Do you have people you can call at 2am?
   - Do you feel less alone?
   - Are you helping others?

### 6.2 Quarterly Check-In

**Every 3 months, ask:**

1. Is my True Baseline still covered?
2. Is my Circle stronger or weaker?
3. Am I less afraid than 3 months ago?
4. What have I learned?
5. What needs to change?

**If answers are mostly positive—you're succeeding.**
