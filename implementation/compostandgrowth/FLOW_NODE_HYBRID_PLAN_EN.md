# FLOW NODE HYBRID – SELF-SUFFICIENT BUILD PLAN

**For 500 people – Three node types with specialized production and realistic resources**

---

## 1. Node Types and Production

### Urban Node (500 m², Automation Level 3)
- **Protein production:** mushrooms, microgreens, leafy greens
- **Energy demand:** 144,400 kWh/year
- **Energy production:** 416,100 kWh/year (50 solar panels × 50 kWh/day, LED optimization)
- **Water:** 80,000 L/year (recirculating hydroponics)
- **Labor:** 520 hours/year (1 hour/person/year)
- **Comments:** High automation compensates for small urban space.

### Peri-Urban Node (2 ha agroforestry, Automation Level 2)
- **Protein production:** nut trees, legumes, berries, mushroom logs
- **Energy demand:** 520,000 kWh/year
- **Energy production:** 1,095,000 kWh/year (solar + wind, partial biomass)
- **Water:** 95,672 L/year (soil recirculation + rainwater harvesting)
- **Labor:** 600 hours/year (1.2 hours/person/year)
- **Comments:** Medium-intensity production, leverages natural ecosystem services.

### Regional Node (1,000 m², Automation Level 4)
- **Protein production:** spirulina, lentils, mung beans
- **Energy demand:** 1,190,985 kWh/year
- **Energy production:** 2,190,000 kWh/year (100 solar panels × 50 kWh/day + 50 wind turbines × 20 kWh/day)
- **Water:** 100,000 L/year (controlled hydroponics + recirculation)
- **Labor:** 542 hours/year (1.08 hours/person/year)
- **Comments:** Fully automated high-energy production, located where energy is abundant and cheap.

---

## 2. Total Protein and Labor

- **Total protein production:** 20,200 kg/year  
- **Required for 500 people:** 13,140 kg/year  
- **Buffer:** ~50% surplus  
- **Total labor:** 1,662 hours/year (~3.3 hours/person/year, <4 minutes/week)  

**Implications:** Buffer allows for experimentation, crop failure, exports, or partial animal feed integration.

## 4. Water Requirements

- **Total:** 275,672 L/year (~550 L/person/year)  
- **Management:** Recirculating systems ensure water sufficiency; water is not limiting.

---

## 5. Inter-Node Flow Strategy

- **Urban:** Low-energy protein (mushrooms, microgreens, leafy greens); energy stored short-term.  
- **Peri-Urban:** Medium-energy protein (nuts, legumes, berries, mushroom logs); thermal + battery storage.  
- **Regional:** High-energy protein (spirulina, lentils, mung beans); battery + hydrogen storage for long-term and exports.  

**Principles:**  
1. Urban nodes maximize high-value, fast-growing protein.  
2. Peri-urban nodes leverage land and agroforestry for stable production.  
3. Regional nodes produce energy-intensive crops where energy is abundant.  
4. Energy and protein surplus flow between nodes as needed.

---

| Level | Description |

## 7. Recommendations & Next Steps

1. Prioritize mushrooms and legumes in low-energy nodes.  
2. Spirulina and other high-energy crops in surplus-energy nodes.  
3. Maximize LED efficiency and automation.  
4. Implement inter-node trading protocols for protein and energy.  
5. Continuous monitoring of energy, water, and labor.  
6. Run three parallel pilot nodes: urban, peri-urban, regional.  
7. Measure, validate, and optimize production, storage, and labor.

## 8. Key Considerations for Real-World Implementation

- **Costs:** Include CAPEX per node (equipment, installation, land) and OPEX per year (energy, maintenance, labor, consumables).  
- **Energy Storage:** Batteries for short-term, hydrogen or other chemical storage for long-term/export.  
- **Labor Management:** Decide how 1,662 hours/year are distributed, roles, and governance.  
- **Data Validation:** Ensure all energy and water figures are based on real-world data, not theoretical.  
- **Inter-Node Protocols:** Define rules for surplus distribution and fair sharing of resources.

## 9. Conclusion

This hybrid Flow Node plan is a fully integrated, realistic, and scalable strategy for a self-sufficient community. It combines:

- Urban, peri-urban, and regional specialization  
- Energy surplus with practical storage solutions  
- Low labor requirements  
- Robust protein and water production  

It provides a blueprint that can be piloted, measured, and scaled, with flexibility to adapt to local conditions and expand into a cooperative node network.
