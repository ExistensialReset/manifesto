## 1. PURPOSE

This document outlines the **global organization of Flow Nodes**, covering:  

- Modular Node design for self-sufficiency  
- Water and wastewater management  
- Energy, food, and Green Labs integration  
- Resource buffers (30 % above needs)  
- Global scalability and environmental sustainability  
- Cost estimates per Node  

---

## 2. PRINCIPLES

- **Self-sufficiency first:** Each Node produces 100 % of its needs + 30 % buffer  
- **Cyclic and sustainable systems:** Water, waste, energy, and food are recycled locally  
- **Environmental consciousness:** Renewable energy, biodegradable products, no harmful chemicals  
- **AI management:** Flow coordinates production, buffers, and redistribution globally  
- **Scalability and modularity:** Nodes adapt to population and regional conditions  
- **Universal accessibility:** All ages, children, elderly, and disabled accommodated

## 4. RESOURCE BUFFER PRINCIPLE

- All Nodes maintain **30 % buffer over daily needs**

## 5. FLOW NETWORK AND AI INTEGRATION

- AI monitors **resource levels, water quality, energy production, medical supply levels**  
- Ensures **own needs + 30 % buffer** are always met before redistribution  
- Coordinates between **Local Nodes → Regional Nodes → Hub Nodes**  
- Redistributes **only surplus resources** to other Nodes in case of shortages  

---

## 6. REDUNDANCY AND RESILIENCY

- Multiple reservoirs and tanks for water and energy  
- Mobile backup modules for water treatment and Green Labs  
- Regional Nodes can support Local Nodes during emergencies  
- 30 % buffer ensures self-sufficiency for short-term disruptions
