## MAJOR CHANGES (6 updates)

### 1. ⭐⭐⭐ LEVEL 6 REVERSAL (Critical)

**OLD STRUCTURE:**
- Transparent Individual Visibility = primary approach
- Anonymous Capacity Display = alternative

**NEW STRUCTURE:**
- **Level 6A: Anonymous Capacity Display = PRIMARY**
  - Shows gap ("Need 20, have 15, gap 5")
  - NO individual names shown
  - Gentler, safer, privacy-protecting

- **Level 6B: Individual Visibility = ABSOLUTE LAST RESORT**
  - Shows names of participants + opt-outs
  - Requires 90% community consent (raised from 75%)
  - Only after 6A tried and failed
  - Heavy monitoring for psychological harm
  - Immediate halt if distress detected

**WHY:**
DeepSeek's scenario:
> "Kalle has social anxiety. He opts out. His name appears. Shame grows. He leaves community."

This is exactly what Flow tries to prevent. Anonymous protects Kalle while still creating awareness of gap.

**IMPACT:**
- Protects people with social anxiety, shame sensitivity, invisible disabilities
- Prevents "us vs them" dynamics
- Still creates accountability through gap visibility
- Much safer psychologically

### 2. ⭐⭐ SPECIALIST OPT-OUT PROTOCOL (New Section)

**Added: Section 4.6.3 - Specialist Opt-Out Protocol (Special Case)**

**Problem identified by DeepSeek:**
> "If we only have one electrician, and electrician is exhausted, can she opt out? In theory yes, but practically it could be catastrophic."

**Solution:**

**Specialist opt-out is PERMITTED** (Flow never coerces):
- Burnout is real
- Forcing continuation causes harm
- Voluntary principle holds even for critical specialists

**But requires explicit plan:**

1. **Immediate coverage:**
   - Regional specialist pool
   - External support (paid professional)
   - Temporary pause of non-critical work

2. **Short-term (1-3 months):**
   - Apprentice fast-track
   - Specialist reduces to part-time (50% load)
   - Community adjusts expectations

3. **Long-term (3-6 months):**
   - Full specialist training for replacement
   - Regional rotation system
   - Redundancy created

**Communication template:**
- Specialist: "I need to step back for [duration] because [burnout/illness/crisis]"
- Community: "Thank you for your service. We'll handle this. Please rest."

**Never acceptable:**
- "You're the only electrician, you can't leave" (coercion)
- Guilting specialist for node's lack of redundancy

**The failure is systemic (no backup), not individual (specialist burned out).**

### 3. ⭐⭐ POSITIVE RECOGNITION WARNING (New)

**Added to Section 4.2.2: ⚠️ WARNING: Positive Recognition Can Become Negative**

**Problem identified by DeepSeek:**
> "There's a risk Level 2 (recognition) glides into Level 6 (visibility/pressure) without anyone noticing. If 'thanking' becomes so intense that non-thanked feel excluded, you've created social control."

**Warning signs:**
- Same 5-10 people thanked every week
- Others stop volunteering ("the heroes already do it")
- Thanked people feel trapped ("I can't stop, I'm 'the bathroom person'")
- Jokes about "those who never help"

**Mitigation strategies:**

1. **Rotate recognition focus:**
   - Week 1: Sanitation team
   - Week 2: Food prep
   - Week 3: Invisible contributions
   - Week 4: Rarely-recognized individuals

2. **Actively look for invisible work:**
   - Who cleaned without being asked?
   - Who quietly fixed things?
   - Who listened during crisis?

3. **Avoid "Hall of Fame" mentality:**
   - No permanent "top contributor" displays
   - No cumulative tracking
   - Keep ephemeral (this week, this month)

4. **Name the pattern:**
   - "We notice we keep thanking same people. Let's spread recognition."

**Goal:** Recognition should include, not create new hierarchy.

### 4. ⭐ FREE RIDER LANGUAGE (Softened)

**OLD:**
> "For 'don't want to' reasons..."
> "actually just don't want to (this is ALSO valid, but different response needed)"

**NEW:**

**Expanded reasons:**
- Need for autonomy
- Different contribution style (invisible work)
- Recovery from burnout
- Personal circumstances unknown to others
- Simply not resonating with structured contribution

**All legitimate. Baseline remains guaranteed.**

**WHY:**
DeepSeek: "Risk of creating 'vi och dom' feeling. Those who don't participate are also part of community."

### 5. ⭐ GENDER-AWARE ROTATION (New Section)

**Added to Section 5.3: ⚠️ Note: Gender-Aware Rotation**

**Problem identified by DeepSeek:**
> "Historically 'invisible work' (especially care work) has been done by women. When making this visible, risk of unconsciously recreating old patterns if not careful."

**Monitor for gendered patterns:**
- Only women doing childcare?
- Only men doing technical/heavy work?
- Care tasks (cleaning, emotional support) mostly women?
- "Leadership" coordination mostly men?

**Intervention when patterns emerge:**

1. **Name the pattern** (no blame):
   > "All childcare volunteers this month are women. This might be unconscious bias. Let's actively invite everyone."

2. **Actively encourage diversity:**
   - Personal invitations to underrepresented genders
   - Highlight when people break stereotypes
   - Value all work equally (care = building = technical)

3. **Examine task framing:**
   - Is childcare described as "nurturing" (coded feminine)?
   - Is building described as "strong work" (coded masculine)?
   - **Reframe neutrally:** Childcare = "supervision and activity facilitation"

4. **Rotation as experimentation:**
   - Everyone tries all types of work
   - Discover unexpected aptitudes
   - Break stereotypes through experience

**Not coercion, but conscious culture-building.**

- Contingency paths
