## 1. PURPOSE

This document estimates **setup costs, operating costs, and expected yields** for hydroponic and local food production across major world regions.  
It is intended to provide a planning basis for self-sufficiency within Flow Nodes.

---

## 2. ASSUMPTIONS

- Hydroponic systems produce vegetables, leafy greens, microalgae, legumes (lentils), and herbs.  
- Local traditional agriculture supplements hydroponics with grains, tubers, and fruit.  
- Node sizes vary; calculations are scaled per 10,000 people per region.  
- All costs are expressed in USD.  
- Yield estimates are conservative, based on current hydroponics research and regional agricultural data.

## 5. NOTES

- Hydroponics allows **higher yield per area** and year-round production, reducing dependency on imports.  
- Inclusion of legumes and microalgae improves **protein diversity** and **nutritional resilience**.  
- Costs include infrastructure, energy, water recycling, nutrient solutions, and labor for management.  
- Yields are **conservative estimates**; modern vertical farms and optimized hydroponics can exceed these numbers.  
- Self-sufficiency depends on **regional coordination** via Flow for distribution and exchange of surplus.

---

## 6. RECOMMENDATIONS

- Prioritize **hydroponics for high-value and perishable crops**.  
- Combine with **traditional local crops** to ensure calorie and protein sufficiency.  
- Use Flow coordination nodes to **optimize surplus flows and minimize waste**.  
- Track yields and costs per Node annually to **refine budget and projections**.
