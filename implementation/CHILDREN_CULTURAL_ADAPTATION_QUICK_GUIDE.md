## UNIVERSAL PRINCIPLES (NON-NEGOTIABLE)

**These apply in EVERY culture, NO exceptions:**

| Principle | Meaning | No Cultural Exception |
|-----------|---------|----------------------|
| **No Child Marriage** | Under 18 years | Even if traditional, not acceptable |
| **No Child Labor (Exploitation)** | Required for survival, excessive hours, dangerous | Family contribution OK; exploitation never |
| **No Physical Violence** | Hitting, beating as discipline | Alternative methods required |
| **No FGM / Forced Body Modification** | Genital cutting, binding, forced procedures | Alternative ceremonies can be created |
| **No Sexual Exploitation** | Any form | Absolute prohibition |
| **Education Access** | Basic literacy, skills, development | Form varies; access doesn't |
| **Baseline Security** | Food, water, shelter, healthcare, safety | Unconditional, always |

## CULTURALLY FLEXIBLE AREAS

**These CAN be adapted to local culture:**

✅ **Family Structure** (nuclear, extended, village, tribal)  
✅ **Age of Responsibility** (different coming-of-age markers, gradual transitions)  
✅ **Gender Roles** (if flexible, chosen, not hierarchical/oppressive)  
✅ **Discipline Methods** (if not violent or traumatic)  
✅ **Education Forms** (formal school, apprenticeship, oral tradition, land-based)  
✅ **Sexuality Discussion** (openness vs conservatism, method varies)  
✅ **Decision-Making** (individual vs collective, with child's voice heard)  
✅ **Coming-of-Age Rituals** (if safe, voluntary, meaningful to child)

## QUICK DISTINCTION: FAMILY CONTRIBUTION VS EXPLOITATION

| Scenario | Acceptable? |
|----------|------------|
| 10-year-old helps herd animals 2 hours, enjoys it, can stop | ✅ YES (voluntary, limited, skill-building) |
| 10-year-old MUST work 8 hours, family starves without it | ❌ NO (required for survival = exploitation) |
| 14-year-old apprentices with elder 3 hours/day, can quit | ✅ YES (voluntary, learning, autonomous) |
| 14-year-old works in factory to pay family debt | ❌ NO (coerced, dangerous, not for child's benefit) |

**Rule:** If Baseline depends on child's work → Exploitation. If child chooses freely, limited hours, safe → Contribution.

## ECONOMIC SUPPORT IS ESSENTIAL

**Why harmful practices persist:** Often economic desperation, not cruelty.

**Flow's response:**
1. **Baseline for all families** (removes survival pressure)
2. **Education stipends** (covers opportunity cost of child's labor)
3. **Microfinancing** (income alternatives for families)
4. **Alternative livelihoods** (for practitioners of harmful traditions)
5. **Community development** (infrastructure, agriculture, market access)
6. **Emergency funds** (prevent crisis-driven harmful choices)

**Principle:** Economic support is not bribery. It is justice. Without it, child protection is just colonialism.

## ADAPTATION PROCESS (5 STEPS)

1. **Identify Cultural Values** (childhood definition, family structure, gender roles, discipline, education, work traditions)
2. **Map Against Universal Principles** (does culture practice child marriage, exploitation, violence, FGM, deny education?)
3. **Adapt Flexible Elements** (how will Baseline work? education form? age structure? gender handling?)
4. **Community Dialogue** (adults, elders, children, practitioners—everyone at table)
5. **Pilot & Iterate** (start with universals, test adaptations, monitor children's wellbeing with their input, adjust continuously)

**Critical:** Include children's voices age-appropriately throughout.

## SPECIAL POPULATIONS

### Children with Disabilities
- Full Baseline regardless of cultural beliefs about disability
- If belief leads to neglect → intervention required
- Respect spiritual meaning, ensure practical care

### Children in Crisis (War, Refugees, Disasters)
- Baseline adapted to emergency (survival first)
- Protection from trafficking, child soldiers, exploitation paramount
- Education/development when possible, not at expense of survival
- Community care for orphans (no institutionalization)

## RED FLAGS: WHEN "CULTURE" BECOMES HARM

**These phrases are warning signs:**
- "You don't understand our culture" (used to shut down child protection)
- "This is how we've always done it" (tradition ≠ morality)
- "Children are different here" (no, children are children everywhere)
- "Western values don't apply" (child protection is universal)

**Risk assessment questions:**
- Does this cause physical harm? → Not acceptable
- Does this traumatize psychologically? → Not acceptable
- Does this deny Baseline? → Not acceptable
- Does this prevent child from leaving/refusing? → Coercion, not acceptable
- Can child consent (age-appropriately)? → If no, reassess

---

## WHEN TO INTERVENE VS DIALOGUE

**Immediate intervention:**
- Child in imminent danger (violence, trafficking, severe neglect)
- Child denied medical care for treatable condition
- FGM or forced marriage

**Dialogue first:**
- Cultural practices potentially harmful but not immediately life-threatening
- Families resisting girls' education
- Physical discipline (not severe)
- Gender limitations (don't deny Baseline)

**Ongoing support, no intervention:**
- Cultural practices different but not harmful
- Traditional education that works
- Gender roles (flexible, chosen)
- Spiritual practices (meaningful to child)

## CASE STUDIES (LEARN FROM SUCCESS)

**Tostan (Senegal) - Anti-FGM:**
- Community-led education, alternative ceremonies, economic support for former practitioners → 8,000+ communities abandoned FGM

**Girls Not Brides (India) - Anti-Child Marriage:**
- Education stipends, girls' empowerment, economic incentives → Child marriage declined 47% → 23% over 15 years

**Plan International (West Africa) - Child Labor:**
- Cooperative farming, school access, economic support → Child labor reduced 60%, school enrollment increased

**Flow Lessons:** Community-led + Economic support + Alternative rituals + Patience (5-10 years) = Sustainable change
