# FLOW_HUMAN_INFRASTRUCTURE_HWA.md
*Human Work Architecture – Rotating Task System*

---

## 1. Purpose
- Describe the **rotating work system for Flow**, integrating legitimate professions, semi-legit roles, and full rotation for all other tasks.
- Audience:
  - New participants in Flow Nodes
  - Node organizers
  - Lyceum Musaeum educators
  - External reviewers

---

## 2. Principles
- **All tasks rotate** among participants, except for fully legitimated professions.
- **Baseline access** (food, housing, energy, healthcare, tools) remains guaranteed, independent of work contribution.
- **Work hours** tracked for learning, contribution mapping, and fairness—not survival.
- **Safety and mentoring** prioritized for physically or cognitively demanding tasks.

---

## 3. Legitimate Professions
These are professions requiring formal certification or licensing; work is **not rotated**:

- Doctors / Nurses / Midwives
- Psychologists / Clinical Social Workers / Licensed Therapists
- Physiotherapists
- Electricians
- Civil Engineers / Infrastructure Designers
- Laboratory specialists (Green Labs: medicine, insulin, hygiene, sanitation)

**Training:** New entrants to these professions are educated via **Lyceum Musaeum**, including mentorship and structured knowledge transfer.

---

## 4. Semi-Legit Professions
- Roles requiring experience, supervision, or specific skills, but not formal licensing.  
- Work may rotate **under supervision or partial rotation**:

- Teachers / Educators (semi-legit)
- Junior lab technicians
- Apprentice engineers / builders
- Node coordinators for specialized tasks

**Rotation:** Semi-legit roles rotate as capacity allows, maintaining safety and quality.

---

## 5. Fully Rotating Tasks
- All other tasks, including but not limited to:

  - Food preparation, gardening, hydroponics
  - Cleaning, maintenance, logistics
  - Non-specialized construction, assembly
  - Peer mentoring and administrative support (non-legit roles)

- **Rotation ensures fairness** and **prevents burnout**.
- Hours tracked for experience mapping and community balance.
- Safety protocols maintained through mentorship and team oversight.

---

## 6. Lyceum Musaeum – Education Hub
- Central hub for **training new legit and semi-legit participants**.
- Functions:

  - Practical workshops for specialized professions
  - Rotating task simulation for general participants
  - Mentorship from certified professionals
  - Knowledge documentation and peer learning
  - Evaluation of readiness for full responsibility

- **Integration with Flow:** Lyceum ensures participants progress from rotated tasks to competent semi-legit or legit roles over time.

---

## 7. Human Work Architecture (HWA) – System Overview
- **Objective:** Integrate baseline, rotating tasks, semi-legit training, and legit professions into one coherent system.
- **Safety & oversight:** Mentors or certified professionals supervise tasks with higher risk.
- **Documentation:** Hours, task outcomes, and rotation logs recorded in Flow Spiral.

---

## 8. Key Rules
1. **Rotation is universal** except for fully legit professions.  
2. **Safety first:** heavy or risky tasks require mentorship.  
3. **Baseline guaranteed:** no access is conditional on work participation.  
4. **Progression:** participants can move from rotating tasks → semi-legit → legit roles via Lyceum.  
5. **Monitoring:** hours and rotations feed into Flow Spiral for feedback and optimization.  
6. **Diversity of experience:** all participants rotate through a wide spectrum of tasks to maintain skill breadth.
