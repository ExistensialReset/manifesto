## 1. PURPOSE

This document defines how hydroponic production and resource flows are integrated within Flow Nodes. The goal is to enable sustainable, local/regional production without currency, creating predictable flows that support the Baseline.

---

## 2. CORE MECHANISMS

### 2.1 Baseline – Automatic Access

**No applications. No bureaucracy.**

Each person automatically has access to:  

- Food (local hydroponic production and Node distribution)  
- Housing (Node architecture)  
- Energy (local production)  
- Healthcare (professional teams)  
- Tools & equipment (Lyceum workshops)

**Mechanism:**  
- Go to distribution hub and take food  
- Live in Node housing  
- Use Lyceum facilities freely  
- Book appointments with professional teams as needed  

No payment. No debt.

---

### 2.2 Work – Contribution, Not Salary

**Why people work:**

Not for survival (Baseline guarantees it), but for:  

- **Meaning** – contributing to something larger  
- **Mastery** – developing competence  
- **Relationship** – belonging to a community  
- **Curiosity** – exploring and creating  

**Examples:**  
- Doctors work to help people  
- Carpenters work because building is meaningful  
- Researchers explore because they are curious  
- Artists create because they must  

No coercion. Just motivation.

---

### 2.3 Specialization – Regional Coordination Without Currency

**Problem:**  
> "How is specialization organized economically – shoemakers, electronics, medicine?"

**Solution:**

#### Local Level
- Shoemakers in each Node receive materials from regional production  
- Access tools via Lyceum  
- Repair and create for everyone  
- Work for meaning, not pay  

#### Regional Level
- Factories produce basic models  
- Receive raw materials from nearby Nodes  
- Distribute to local specialists  
- No sales – only distribution  

**Motivation:**  
1. Baseline covers all survival needs  
2. Work gives meaning and community  
3. Specialization provides status and mastery  
4. Nodes recognize contributions socially

---

### 2.4 Regional & Global Resource Exchange

**15% regional, 5% global resources**  

#### Regional Exchange (15%)
- Node A produces grapes → wine  
- Node B produces grains → bread  
- Node C produces fish  

**Exchange Mechanism:**  
- Node A sends wine to B and C  
- Node B sends grains to A and C  
- Node C sends fish to A and B  

Coordination nodes track flows; equilibrium maintained organically over time.  

#### Global Exchange (5%)
- Coffee, tea, spices, rare minerals  

**Example:**  
- Europe → machinery to South America  
- South America → coffee to Europe  
- Asia → electronics to Africa  
- Africa → cocoa to Asia  

Global Flow network tracks flows long-term, focusing on reciprocity, not profit.

---

### 2.5 Professional Teams – Why Do They Work?

**Question:** If no one pays, why do doctors, nurses, psychologists, and other specialists work?

**Answer:**  

#### A) Baseline Applies
- Food, housing, tools, and leisure are guaranteed  

#### B) Status & Meaning Replace Salary
- Being an expert, helping others, and earning community respect  

#### C) Education is Free
- Lyceum and integrated universities provide free training  
- People choose their profession out of desire, not debt repayment

---

### 2.6 Quality Assurance

**Professionals:**  
- Certification and peer review  
- Patients can choose another professional  
- Nodes can rotate out incompetents  

**Craftspeople:**  
- Reputation and mastery drive quality, not money  
- Social recognition and community feedback ensure standards

---

### 2.7 Non-Contributors

**Question:** Can someone take the Baseline and contribute nothing?  

**Answer:** Yes. But:  
1. Most people want to contribute when not stressed  
2. Baseline ensures survival; more complex life requires participation  
3. Community provides belonging; isolation is painful  
4. Nodes can apply soft social nudges over time  

**Example:**  
- Year 1: Person rests, community observes  
- Year 2: Community notices inactivity  
- Year 3: Gentle prompt: "Would you like to join in creating something?"  

No exclusion, only social dynamics.
