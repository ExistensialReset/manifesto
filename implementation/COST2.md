## 1. PURPOSE

This document provides a global cost estimate for establishing Node-level hydroponic systems within Flow. Costs include infrastructure, equipment, labor, and initial consumables for first-year operation.

---

| Region | Infrastructure & Equipment | Labor & Training | First-Year Consumables | Total Estimated Cost (USD) |

| South America | $180,000 | $40,000 | $20,000 | **$240,000** |

**Notes:**  
- Infrastructure & Equipment: Modular racks, water/nutrient tanks, LED lighting, climate control systems, IoT monitoring.  
- Labor & Training: Hydroponic technicians, agronomists, maintenance staff, community workshops.  
- First-Year Consumables: Nutrients, seeds, algae starter cultures, water filtration supplies.  

---

## 3. SCALABILITY

- **Per Node:** Figures above assume ~500 m² hydroponic area per Node.  
- **Regional Scaling:** Costs scale approximately linearly; regional hubs may reduce redundancy by ~10–15% if multiple Nodes share supply chains.  
- **Global Implementation:** Full deployment across all Nodes globally would require multiplying per-Node costs by the number of Nodes in each region.

---

## 4. RISK & VARIABILITY

- Equipment shipping and import tariffs may increase infrastructure costs in remote regions.  
- Local labor availability affects staffing costs.  
- Energy costs (LED lighting, pumps) vary by country; renewable energy integration can reduce long-term operational costs.  
- Consumable pricing subject to crop type, algae culture, and nutrient sourcing.

---

## 5. SUMMARY

Hydroponic Node setup is capital-intensive but **rapidly scalable** and **baseline-guaranteed**.  
Approximate per-Node costs range from **$180,000** (Sub-Saharan Africa) to **$350,000** (Europe/North America).  
Costs can be amortized over multiple years of production, with first-year output offsetting part of operational expenses.
