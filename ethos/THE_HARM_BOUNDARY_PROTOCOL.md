# THE_HARM_BOUNDARY_PROTOCOL.md

**Status:** ACTIVE / PROTECTIVE  
**Principle:** The victim never moves. The perpetrator always does.  
**Core Axiom:** Flow tolerates all emotions, all failure, all struggle. Flow does not tolerate sadism, violence, or systemic abuse.

---

## §1. DEFINITION OF UNFORGIVABLE HARM

Unforgivable harm is any intentional or systemic crossing of personal boundaries through force, malice, or coercion. This includes:

### 1.1 Sadistic Harm
- **Intentional infliction of suffering** for the perpetrator's gratification, not by accident, miscommunication, or dysregulation.  
- **Sustained pattern** of behavior despite formal notice to cease.  
- **Absence of repair intent** – the person refuses to acknowledge harm, repair damage, or restore trust.

### 1.2 Physical Violence
- **Any use of weapons** against another person.  
- **Sexual assault** of any form.  
- **Severe physical attacks** requiring medical intervention.  
- **Credible threats** to life or bodily integrity.

### 1.3 Digital and Psychological Abuse
- **Doxxing, stalking, sustained harassment, or identity theft**.  
- **Coordinated campaigns** to harm reputation, livelihood, or safety.  
- **Non-consensual dissemination** of intimate or sensitive content.

---

## §2. IMMEDIATE PROTECTIVE RESPONSE

When harm is reported and **preliminarily verified**, the system enacts **Total Separation** within 24 hours:

### Step 1: Protect the Victim
- The victim **remains in place**, with **full baseline support**.  
- **Access to Refugium Anima, trusted witnesses, and advocacy** is optional but offered.  
- **The victim is never forced** to engage with the perpetrator or disclose experience.

### Step 2: Remove the Perpetrator
Perpetrator undergoes **three-fold separation**:

#### Physical Separation
- Immediate relocation to a **Single-Node or isolated Node**.  
- **No contact** with victim’s Circle or region.  
- Temporary GPS monitoring allowed **only for safety**, with **documented human review** before any escalation.  
- **Permanent exile** may only occur after independent proportionality review.

#### Digital Separation
- Block all digital access to victim and related networks.  
- Attempts to circumvent access result in **temporary containment** pending review, not immediate exile.  

#### Social Separation
- Perpetrator’s presence is removed from victim-related records.  
- Victim **never required** to acknowledge perpetrator.  
- Circle acts as witness and supports victim narrative.

---

## §3. PERPETRATOR PATHWAYS

### Option A: Acknowledgment & Transformation (Rare)
- Full acknowledgment of harm, acceptance of separation, and commitment to **2+ years verified healing work**.  
- Supervised participation in **remote Circle only**, full disclosure mandatory.  
- Any relapse triggers **proportionate review**; permanent exile only after independent verification.

### Option B: Refusal or Repeat Offense
- Denial of harm, boundary violations, or new harm triggers **permanent containment**:
  - Physical: isolated Node.  
  - Digital: full network exclusion.  
  - Social: removal from all Circles.  
- **Baseline preserved** (food, housing, healthcare).  
- **Appeal possible** via proportionality review for reclassification, not for victim contact.

---

## §4. WEAPON VIOLENCE AND SEVERE HARM

- Weapons or credible life-threatening acts **trigger immediate protective separation**.  
- **No irreversible permanent exile** without **documented independent review**, contextual assessment, and appeal pathway.  
- Temporary containment allows rapid protection **while proportionality review is conducted**.  

---

## §5. PROTECTION AGAINST FALSE ACCUSATIONS

- **Evidence threshold:** physical proof, corroborating witnesses, pattern documentation.  
- **Sadistic Harm:** requires multiple independent corroborations **plus independent proportionality review**.  
- Temporary separation may occur **safely and proportionally** during investigation.  
- False accusations trigger the **same separation process**, proportionate to intent and impact.

---

## §6. VICTIM’S ABSOLUTE RIGHTS

1. **No forced contact**: ever.  
2. **No demanded forgiveness**: never required.  
3. **Veto power**: absolute over any re-entry petition.  
4. **Healing timeline controlled by victim**.

---

## §7. ENFORCEMENT PRINCIPLES

- Safety first, baseline preserved.  
- Separation **not punishment**; distance enforces boundaries.  
- Independent review and proportionality are **mandatory** for irreversible consequences.  
- Harsher measures apply **only to confirmed, repeated, or weaponized harm**.

---

## §8. FINAL PRINCIPLE

**The victim never moves. The perpetrator always does.**  
**Harmful acts = immediate protective separation.**  
**Confirmed or repeated weaponized acts = permanent containment**, following independent review.
