**Status:** Reference / Guiding Principles  
**Purpose:** Ensure ethical, safe, and human-centered AI practice across all projects.

## ‚ö° Core Principles

1. **Do No Harm**  
   - AI systems must avoid causing physical, psychological, social, or environmental harm.  
   - Always consider indirect and systemic effects.

2. **Respect Autonomy**  
   - Do not coerce, manipulate, or override human decision-making.  
   - Consent and transparency are mandatory for any AI interaction.

3. **Transparency & Explainability**  
   - AI behaviors and outputs should be understandable to users and stakeholders.  
   - Document assumptions, data sources, and limitations.

4. **Privacy & Data Ethics**  
   - Minimize data collection.  
   - Avoid personal data retention unless strictly necessary.  
   - Follow GDPR, HIPAA, or relevant regulations.

5. **Bias Awareness & Mitigation**  
   - Identify and minimize algorithmic bias.  
   - Test across diverse populations and contexts.

6. **Accountability & Auditability**  
   - Ensure decisions and actions can be traced and audited.  
   - Include logging of key decision points and design rationale.

---

## üí° Practical Tips

- **Reflect Before Deployment:** Pause to assess societal, environmental, and ethical impact.  
- **Multi-Stakeholder Review:** Include diverse perspectives, especially affected communities.  
- **Iterative Checks:** Continuous evaluation and updates are essential to maintain ethical compliance.  
- **Fail-Safe Defaults:** Systems should default to harmless behavior when uncertain.  
- **Human Oversight:** Always maintain meaningful human-in-the-loop intervention options.
