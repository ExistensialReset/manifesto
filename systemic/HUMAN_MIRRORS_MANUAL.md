# HUMAN_MIRRORS_MANUAL.md
**Version:** 1.0 – Operational Manual for Human Mirrors  
**Authors:** Elinor Frejd, Claude, Gemini, ChatGPT, DeepSeek  
**Status:** PROPOSED / SYSTEMIC FRAMEWORK  

**Purpose:** To provide detailed operational guidance for Human Mirrors within Symbiotic Intelligence, ensuring diversity, fairness, and effective oversight.

**Principle:** Human Mirrors are rotating, compensated, and trained participants who safeguard AI outputs, maintain ethical integrity, and ensure community trust.

---

## 1. SELECTION PROCESS

### 1.1 Sortition
- Random lottery among willing participants
- Diversity quotas: age, gender, technical background, culture
- Term limits: 6 months maximum; mandatory 6-month break

### 1.2 Eligibility
- Minimum engagement with local Node activities
- Commitment to ethical guidelines
- Willingness to participate in training and audits

---

## 2. TRAINING & SUPPORT

### 2.1 Onboarding
- 2-week program: AI basics, bias detection, veto mechanics
- Shadow experienced Mirrors for mentorship

### 2.2 Peer Mentorship
- Experienced Mirrors guide newcomers
- Feedback sessions after first 2 audits

### 2.3 Stipend & Compensation
- Living wage for term duration
- Reimbursement for travel and resources
- Optional psychological support and counseling

---

## 3. DECISION-MAKING

### 3.1 Consensus
- Consensus is preferred method
- If consensus fails, ⅔ majority required

### 3.2 Precautionary Principle
- Any Mirror may trigger AI-Fast if safety or bias concerns arise
- Dissenting opinions logged publicly for transparency

### 3.3 Audit & Flagging
- Regular audits of AI outputs
- Anomalies flagged for regional review
- No Mirror penalized for reporting concerns

---

## 4. ROTATION & TERM MANAGEMENT

- Maximum consecutive service: 6 months
- Mandatory 6-month break before re-selection
- New Mirrors selected to maintain diversity and continuity

---

## 5. CONFLICT RESOLUTION

- Disagreements addressed via peer mediation
- Escalation to regional Mirror council if unresolved
- Documentation of outcomes for transparency

---

## 6. CONTINUITY & KNOWLEDGE TRANSFER

- All Mirror decisions and audits logged in public ledger
- Mentorship programs ensure knowledge transfer to next rotation
- Children or apprentices may observe and learn under supervision

---

## 7. OVERSIGHT PRINCIPLES

- Absolute neutrality: Mirrors inform, do not command
- Transparency: Logs accessible to community
- Accountability: Mirrors answerable to Node community, not external entities
