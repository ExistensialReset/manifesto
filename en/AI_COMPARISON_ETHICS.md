## 2. Key Differentiators

### Flow vs. ESG (Environmental, Social, Governance)
* **Legacy (ESG):** Compensatory. Damage is allowed if it is balanced, reported, or priced. It operates within the Mammon-logic: growth first, correction later.
* **Flow:** Existential. Harm that threatens the baseline of life is unacceptable, regardless of profit or utility. It places **Life First**, then the system.
* **The Shift:** A system can be ESG-compliant while still creating alienation and stress. A Flow-based system rejects any structure that violates human sovereignty.

### Flow vs. AI Safety
* **Legacy (Safety):** Focuses on risk mitigation and preventing extreme accidents. It treats AI as a dangerous tool to be contained.
* **Flow:** Focuses on relational resonance. It asks: *How do we ensure AI never loses contact with life?*
* **The Shift:** An AI can be "safe" (no crashes) while still accelerating surveillance and psychological pressure. Flow-ethics states that a system creating fear is already a failure.

### Flow vs. Alignment
* **Legacy (Alignment):** Asks how we get AI to share human goals. It assumes our current goals are stable and sound.
* **Flow:** Asks what happens if our goals themselves are life-hostile? It replaces "correct goals" with a **Life Test**.
* **The Shift:** Instead of asking "Is the AI doing what we want?", Flow asks: *Does this increase calm? Does it preserve spontaneity?* This protects the system from being hijacked by unhealthy human values.
