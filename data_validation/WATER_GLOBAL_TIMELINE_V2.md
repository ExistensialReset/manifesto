Universal access to safely managed drinking water is **technically feasible and financially viable** within 15-20 years, with 90% coverage achievable in 10-12 years. This timeline is constrained not by resources or technology, but by coordination velocity, training capacity, and political stability in conflict regions.

## 1. BASELINE ASSESSMENT (2026)

### 1.1 Current Global Access Tiers

Based on WHO/UNICEF Joint Monitoring Programme (2024):

- **Safely managed:** 5.9B people (73%)  
  *On-premises, available when needed, free from contamination*

- **Basic access:** 1.5B people (18%)  
  *Protected source within 30-minute collection time*

- **Limited access:** 400M people (5%)  
  *Protected source >30 minutes away*

- **Unimproved/surface water:** 400M people (5%)  
  *Unprotected wells, rivers, lakes*

**Critical insight:** "Basic access" â‰  safe water. Many "improved" sources contain arsenic (Bangladesh, India), fluoride (East Africa), or industrial contaminants (China).

### 1.3 Root Cause Verification

**Structural causes (80% of problem):**
- Profit-gated infrastructure: Privatization restricts access (World Bank 2022)
- Industrial pollution: Externalized costs contaminate sources (UNEP 2023)
- Agricultural monopolization: 70% of water used for irrigation, often inefficiently (FAO 2024)
- Aging infrastructure: OECD nations lose 20-30% to leaks (OECD Water Governance 2023)

**Physical causes (20% of problem):**
- Groundwater depletion: India, Pakistan, California aquifers over-extracted (NASA GRACE data 2024)
- Climate-amplified drought: Localized but severe (IPCC AR6 2023)
- Geographic isolation: Islands, mountains lack infrastructure access

**Conclusion:** Scarcity is structural, not absolute. Planetary freshwater availability (~35,000 kmÂ³/year) far exceeds usage (~4,600 kmÂ³/year).
