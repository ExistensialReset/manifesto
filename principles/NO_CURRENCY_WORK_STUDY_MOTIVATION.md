### 1. PURPOSE

This document explores whether people will **work and pursue education without monetary compensation**, using data, historical examples, and intrinsic motivation research. It provides realistic probabilities and scenarios for a Flow-like system.

---

### 2. WHY PEOPLE WORK: INTRINSIC MOTIVATION

#### Self-Determination Theory (Deci & Ryan, 2000)
Three fundamental psychological needs:
1. **Autonomy** – control over one’s actions  
2. **Competence** – sense of skill and mastery  
3. **Relatedness** – connection with others  

**When these are met:** Intrinsic motivation arises.  
**When unmet:** Financial compensation is often required.

#### Daniel Pink, "Drive" (2009)
For knowledge workers, motivation comes from:
- **Autonomy** – freedom to choose  
- **Mastery** – improving skills  
- **Purpose** – meaningful work  

**Money only motivates up to a certain threshold** (~$100k/year equivalent, 2026).

### 3. DATA FROM REAL-WORLD EXAMPLES

| Example | Pay | Retention | Motivation |
|---------|-----|-----------|------------|
| **PhD students (USA)** | $25-35k/yr | 55-60% complete | Passion, intellectual stimulation, future career |
| **Doctors Without Borders** | $0-2k/mo | 15% >2yr | Helping people, purpose, experience |
| **Open Source Developers** | $0 | 5% fulltime | Learning, reputation, community contribution |
| **Kibbutzim (Israel)** | Minimal | 70-80% peak, 40% later | Ideology, survival, community |
| **Mondragon Co-ops (Spain)** | Limited pay ratio | 70-80% | Ownership, democracy, fairness |

**Lessons:**  
- Work without money is possible **if meaningful, visible, and community-supported**.  
- Longer or more complex roles (e.g., doctors) require additional incentives beyond Baseline.

### 4. FLOW SYSTEM ASSUMPTIONS

- **Baseline guaranteed**: food, water, housing, energy, healthcare  
- **Social Recognition System (SRS)**: contribution points give access to non-essential perks  
- **Work required per Node**: 6–20 hours/week, varying by role  
- **Intrinsic motivators**: purpose, mastery, community, autonomy

### 6. EDUCATION AND PROFESSIONAL TRAINING

**Example: Doctors per 500-person Node**
| Role | Training | Probability | Retention |
|------|----------|------------|-----------|
| Basic healthcare (nurse) | 2 yrs | 85% | 65-75% |
| Teacher (primary) | 2 yrs | 80% | 60-70% |
| Engineer (renewables) | 4 yrs | 70% | 55-65% |
| Advanced doctor | 8 yrs | 40-60% | 35-50% |
| Agricultural specialist | 1 yr | 90% | 80% |
| IT / LOTUS specialist | 3 yrs | 75% | 55-65% |

**Insights:**
- Shorter training → high completion  
- Longer training → lower completion but can be supplemented by **external recruitment** (e.g., burned-out Mammon professionals)  
- Flow population requires only a **tiny fraction of people in training at any time** compared to global capacity  

---

### 7. RETENTION AND SYSTEM SUSTAINABILITY

**Overall retention for essential and care work**:
- 50-70% after 5 years depending on community strength, transparency, ideology, and alternative options.  

**Work distribution**:
- Essential services (food, water, energy, housing): 90-95% probability  
- Care services (children, elderly): 80-85% probability  
- Teaching: 75-80% probability  
- Healthcare (basic nursing): 70-75% probability  
- Advanced healthcare (doctors): 40-60% probability  
- Specialized skills (engineering, IT): 60-70% probability  

**Conclusion:** Flow **does not require 100% participation**. Partial engagement is sufficient due to automation and surplus production.  

---

### 8. KEY FACTORS FOR SUCCESS

1. **Ideology strength** – Early adopters more likely to complete training and stay  
2. **Availability of alternative options** – Mammon opportunities reduce retention  
3. **SRS credibility** – fair recognition increases motivation  
4. **Community strength** – strong Circles support long-term engagement  
5. **Visible impact** – progress that is tangible sustains intrinsic motivation  

---

### 9. SUMMARY

- People **will work and learn without money**, if Baseline is secure and work is meaningful, visible, and socially recognized.  
- Shorter training programs have higher completion and retention; longer ones require additional mechanisms (external recruitment, prestige, purpose).  
- Flow systems are **sustainable with 50-70% retention** and surplus production of 130-150% in essential sectors.  
- Not everyone participates equally, and **variation is normal and acceptable**.
