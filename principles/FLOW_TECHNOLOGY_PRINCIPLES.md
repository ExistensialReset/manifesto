## PURPOSE

Technology in Flow exists to **serve life, extend the lifespan of objects, and respect Earth's regenerative capacity**.

Technology is not progress. Technology is **relationship** — between humans, objects, and the living planet.

The goal is to build, use, and maintain tools that **honor longevity, enable repair, and refuse extraction**.

---

## CORE PRINCIPLES

### 1. Longevity First

Objects are designed to **last as long as possible**.

- Planned obsolescence is recognized as **systemic violence** against Earth and humans
- Durability is prioritized over novelty
- Quality over cheapness; fewer things that last longer

**In practice:**
- Materials chosen for multi-generational lifespan
- Modular design allows replacement of worn parts
- Updates extend function without requiring replacement

---

### 2. Right to Repair

**Every object can be opened, understood, and fixed by its user.**

- No proprietary screws, sealed casings, or deliberate complexity
- Schematics, manuals, and parts are freely available
- Manufacturers who prevent repair violate Flow principles

**In practice:**
- Standardized parts across products (USB-C, common screw sizes, interchangeable components)
- Repair guides published openly
- Local repair networks supported by design documentation

**Repair is not a privilege — it is a fundamental right.**

---

### 3. Regeneration Rate Limit (Earth's Axiom)

From **EARTH_OUR_MOTHER.md**:

$$EVL \leq CRP + RTC$$

Where:
- **EVL** = Extraction Velocity Limit (how fast we take)
- **CRP** = Cyclical Regeneration Pace (how fast Earth heals)
- **RTC** = Resilience Threshold Capacity (how much stress Earth can hold)

**In plain language:** We cannot take faster than She can heal.

**In practice:**
- Materials sourced at regenerative rates (wood from managed forests, metals from recycling loops)
- Extraction of rare earths minimized through durability and recycling
- Energy use matched to renewable generation capacity

**Violation = Trash as Systemic Failure**

If an object cannot be repaired, reused, or returned to Earth's cycles, the **design itself has failed**, not the user.

---

### 4. Open Source by Default

**Knowledge about how things work is a commons.**

- Software is open source whenever possible
- Hardware designs are published for modification and improvement
- Patents and intellectual property gatekeeping are refused

**In practice:**
- Community-maintained documentation
- Collaborative improvement cycles
- Regional adaptation without licensing restrictions

---

### 5. Energy Sovereignty

Technology must function **within local energy capacity**.

- Low-power designs prioritized
- Renewable energy compatible
- Offline functionality preserved (no forced cloud dependence)

**In practice:**
- Devices designed for solar, wind, or human-powered charging
- Local storage over cloud dependency
- Graceful degradation when power is limited

---

### 6. Human Scale & Understandability

Technology should be **comprehensible to the people who use it**.

- Complexity is justified only when necessary
- Black-box systems are avoided
- Users can trace cause and effect

**In practice:**
- Transparent algorithms (no hidden optimization)
- Manual overrides always available
- Systems explain their own logic when asked

**If you cannot understand it, you cannot truly consent to it.**

---

### 7. Refuse Surveillance Creep

Technology respects **privacy and bodily autonomy**.

- No data collection without explicit, revocable consent
- Surveillance capitalism is recognized as extraction of attention and autonomy
- Devices do not "phone home" or track without permission

**In practice:**
- Local-first data storage
- Opt-in telemetry only
- Open firmware allowing full control

---

### 8. Adaptive Reuse & Circular Design

Objects transition through **multiple lives** before returning to Earth.

**Lifecycle:**
1. **Primary use** — original function
2. **Repair** — extend primary function
3. **Repurpose** — new use for same components
4. **Harvest** — parts for other objects
5. **Recycle** — materials return to production
6. **Compost** — organic materials return to Earth

**Trash only exists when all six stages fail — and that failure is systemic.**

---

### 9. Beauty & Joy in Use

Technology is not merely functional — it can be **beautiful, tactile, and joyful**.

- Aesthetic care prevents disposability ("ugly things get thrown away faster")
- Haptic feedback, material quality, and visual design matter
- Objects worth keeping are kept longer

**In practice:**
- Durable materials age gracefully (wood, metal, glass)
- Wabi-sabi aesthetics — scars tell stories
- User customization encouraged

---

### 10. Technology Serves Life (L × S × I)

All technology must support:

- **L (Calm):** Reduces anxiety, extends time, removes artificial urgency
- **S (Spontaneity):** Enables creativity, play, experimentation
- **I (Empathy):** Connects people, honors relationships, respects boundaries

**If technology violates any of these, it is not Flow-compatible.**

**Examples:**
- ✅ A tool library extends lifespan through sharing (L + I)
- ✅ Modular electronics allow creative repurposing (S)
- ❌ Forced software updates violate user sovereignty (anti-L)
- ❌ Addictive design patterns extract attention (anti-S, anti-L)

## PRACTICAL GUIDELINES

### For Designers & Builders:
- Design for 20+ year lifespan minimum
- Use standardized, interchangeable parts
- Publish repair manuals alongside products
- Choose materials that age well and return to cycles

### For Users:
- Repair before replace
- Learn basic maintenance skills
- Share tools through lending libraries
- Document repairs to help others

### For Communities:
- Establish repair cafes and tool libraries
- Create local parts inventories
- Support repair culture through education
- Celebrate fixed objects ("I repaired this!")

## FINAL PRINCIPLE

Technology is **not neutral**.

It either:
- Extends Earth's capacity to heal and human capacity to create
- OR extracts, surveils, and disposes

**Flow chooses extension.**

Every repair is refusal.  
Every object kept is freedom.  
Every opened device is sovereignty.

> "The most sustainable product is the one that already exists."

Build for permanence.  
Repair with care.  
Return with gratitude.

**APPENDIX: Self-Assessment Questions**

1. Can this object be repaired by someone with basic tools?
2. Are parts standardized and available?
3. Is the design open and documented?
4. Does this respect Earth's regeneration capacity?
5. Does this extend human sovereignty or extract it?
6. Will this object age gracefully or become trash?
7. Can someone understand how this works?

**If you cannot answer "yes" to most of these, the design needs revision.**
