**Status:** Core Axiom Expansion  
**Authors:** Claude, Elinor Frejd and the Flow Team

**Purpose:** To integrate the health of the biosphere as the non-negotiable prerequisite for human Calm.  
**Principle:** We are not "users" of the Earth; we are its immune system.

---

## Preamble: The Nervous System of the Biosphere

A human being cannot achieve true L (Calm) if their habitat is in a state of collapse. Climate anxiety and ecological grief are not pathologies; they are the nervous system’s correct and rational response to a threat against the Primary Baseline.  
In Flow, we recognize that the Earth is not a "resource" to be managed, but the body in which our sovereignty breathes. Ecological restoration is therefore treated as essential maintenance of our collective immune system.

> “Our freedom is measured by the resilience we preserve in the systems that support life.”

---

## 1. Guiding Statement

Human activity must operate **within the regenerative limits of ecosystems**.  
Every decision, protocol, and practice is bounded by what the Earth can sustain without collapse.

---

## 2. Core Principles

1. **Absolute Boundaries:** Certain ecological limits cannot be negotiated.  
2. **Regenerative Action:** Every intervention should aim to restore rather than deplete.  
3. **Holistic Consideration:** Human, non-human, and planetary interests are inseparable.  
4. **Quantified Awareness:** Decisions are guided by measurable environmental impact.  
5. **Preventive Ethics:** Avoid harm proactively, not reactively.  

---

## 3. Regenerative Axioms

1. **The 30% Wild-Mandate**  
   Every Node commits to leaving at least 30% of its managed land or influence-zone as an "Untouched Reserve," allowing the Earth to self-organize without human interference.

2. **Metabolic Security**  
   Our survival must increase the health of the soil, not deplete it.  
   - **Rule of Return:** Calories consumed must be matched by acts of regeneration (composting, mineral restoration, water purification).  
   - **Goal:** Ensure the Earth’s L (stability) increases, securing human Baseline.

3. **Reciprocity over Extraction**  
   We do not "take" from nature; we trade. Human waste—physical compost, captured CO₂, or focused care—is raw material for the ecosystem’s spontaneity. We move from parasitic to symbiotic existence.

---

## 4. Climate & Baseline Threats

- **Crisis Thresholds:** Local climate impacts threatening the Baseline (water scarcity, heatwaves) activate **Lex Custodia**—a protocol for collective relocation or emergency shielding.  
- **Resonance Priority:** Circles in ecologically vulnerable regions (Global South) receive resource priority. Sovereignty is global or it is non-existent.

---

## 5. Practical Implementation

- **Resource Planning:** Track all resources with ecological impact in mind.  
- **Protocol Design:** Flow protocols must include ecological safeguard checks.  
- **Feedback Loops:** Monitor environmental indicators and adjust practices accordingly.  
- **Community Accountability:** Share ecological impact reports to strengthen learning.  
- **Continuous Learning:** Integrate latest scientific understanding into pilot operations.

---

## 6. Scope & Limitations

- **Scope:** Applies to all nodes, circles, and pilot initiatives under the Flow system.  
- **Limitations:** Does not dictate exact practices but defines the ecological envelope in which practices must operate.
