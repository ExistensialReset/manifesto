## 1. PURPOSE

This document describes the **global organization of Flow Nodes**:  

- Modular Node design for self-sufficiency  
- Water and wastewater management  
- Energy, food, and Green Labs integration  
- Resource buffers (30 % above needs)  
- Global scalability and environmental sustainability  
- AI used **only as monitoring and advisory tool**, all decisions made by humans  
- **Workforce measured in human hours** (not monetary compensation)  

---

## 2. PRINCIPLES

- **Self-sufficiency first:** Each Node produces 100 % of its own needs + 30 % buffer  
- **Cyclic and sustainable systems:** Water, waste, energy, and food are recycled locally  
- **Human-led management:** Resource allocation, distribution, and priority decisions are made by local teams  
- **AI advisory:** Calculates needs, forecasts risks, and provides suggestions only  
- **Modularity and scalability:** Nodes adapt to population and regional conditions  
- **Universal accessibility:** All ages and abilities accommodated  
- **Labor measured in hours:** All work is counted in **human hours**, not money  

---

## 3. NODE TYPES AND MODULES

| Node Type                     | Core Modules                                                                                      |
|-------------------------------|--------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| **Primary Care Node**          | Health checks, vaccinations, minor illness treatment, local Green Labs module, water/energy      |
| **Specialist Care Node**       | Specialist consultations, lab diagnostics, telemedicine, larger Green Labs, water/energy systems |
| **Emergency / Surgical Node**  | Emergency interventions, surgery, trauma care, advanced Green Labs, water/energy redundancy      |
| **Rehabilitation Node**        | Physical therapy, neurorehabilitation, mobility support, medical aids, water/energy integration  |
| **Regional Green Lab**         | Large-scale production of medicines, hygiene, cleaning products, distribution coordination       |

| Module                      | Function                                                                                     | Human Hours/Day |
|-----------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|----------------|
| **Freshwater Production**   | Rain/river water capture, filtration, UV treatment, renewable energy-driven                  | 4              |

---

### 3.2 Energy & Utilities

- Solar, wind, and biogas-powered systems  
- Energy for water treatment, Green Labs, food production, and Node operations  
- Surplus energy can be shared manually between Nodes  
- Human hours required for maintenance: **5–8 hours per day** per Node  

---

### 3.3 Food & Green Labs

- Hydroponics, microalgae, and other sustainable crops  
- Medicines, hygiene, and cleaning products produced by Green Labs  
- Local food production scaled to Node population + 30 % buffer  
- Only surplus is shared with other Nodes via Flow network  
- Human hours: **variable per crop or lab operation**; e.g., hydroponic farm 10–15 h/day for 500 people  

---

## 4. RESOURCE BUFFER PRINCIPLE

- Nodes maintain **30 % buffer above daily needs**  
- Ensures resilience against:  
  - Supply chain delays  
  - Weather events or natural disruptions  
  - Sudden healthcare demand increases  

| Resource Type        | Daily Need | 30 % Buffer | Total Production Node | Human Hours/Day |
|---------------------|------------|-------------|---------------------|----------------|
| Energy (kWh)        | 100 000    | 30 000      | 130 000             | 5–8            |
| Food (kg)           | 10 000     | 3 000       | 13 000              | 10–15          |
| Medicine (units)    | 50 000     | 15 000      | 65 000              | 4–6            |
| Hygiene & Cleaning  | 5 000      | 1 500       | 6 500               | 2–3            |
| Water (L)           | 1 000 000  | 300 000     | 1 300 000           | 10             |

> **Note:** All human hours are estimated daily averages required to maintain operation. Labor is counted as **human hours**, not monetary payment.  

---

## 5. FLOW NETWORK AND DECISION-MAKING

- **Human teams** monitor resource levels, buffer status, and needs  
- **AI provides:**  
  - Forecasts of water, food, energy, medicine, and Green Labs requirements  
  - Risk alerts for shortages or bottlenecks  
  - Visualizations of Node status and resource flows  
- **Humans decide:**  
  - Resource distribution within Node  
  - When and how to share surplus with other Nodes  
  - Adjustments to production or storage  

---

## 6. REDUNDANCY AND RESILIENCY

- Multiple water and energy reservoirs per Node  
- Mobile backup modules for water treatment and Green Labs  
- Regional Nodes support Local Nodes during emergencies  
- 30 % buffer ensures self-sufficiency for short-term disruptions  
- Offline manuals and human monitoring ensure AI failures do not stop operations  

---

## 7. SCALABILITY AND GLOBAL APPLICABILITY

- Modular design adapts to **different populations and climates**  
- Mobile modules allow deployment in regions without permanent infrastructure  
- Human teams coordinate redistribution, ensuring **fair access without money**  
- Flexible decision-making based on **real-time needs, resources, and buffer levels**, supported but not controlled by AI  

---

## 8. HUMAN HOURS EXPLAINED

- **Definition:** Total time spent per person per day to operate and maintain Node modules  
- **Purpose:**  
  - Plan staffing and rotations  
  - Balance workload fairly  
  - Track effort for specialist tasks  
- **Important:** Human hours are **not wages**. All contributions are voluntary, recognized via status, training, and access to resources.
