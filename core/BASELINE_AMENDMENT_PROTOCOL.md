# BASELINE_AMENDMENT_PROTOCOL.md
## Version 1.0
## Status: CONSTITUTIONAL
## Scope: Global
## Applies To: RESOURCE_METRIC_STANDARDS.md

---

# PURPOSE

This protocol defines how the Global Baseline may be amended.

Baseline is the structural floor of Flow.

It must evolve carefully.
It must not be politically destabilized.

---

# I. AMENDMENT DIRECTIONS

Baseline may be:

• Increased
• Decreased
• Refined (metric clarification without value change)

Each category has different thresholds.

---

# II. INCREASING BASELINE

To raise any Baseline metric:

Requires:

• 66% Global LOTUS supermajority
• 30-day public review period
• AI simulation report (impact forecast)

Increase must specify:

• Effective date
• Transition timeline
• Regional implementation feasibility

Raising Baseline is considered progressive stabilization.

---

# III. DECREASING BASELINE

To reduce any Baseline metric:

Requires:

• 75% Global LOTUS supermajority
• 60-day public review period
• Mandatory dual-AI simulation
• Regional feasibility audit
• Published justification report

Lowering Baseline is classified as structural contraction.

Burden of proof lies with amendment initiators.

---

# IV. TEMPORARY REDUCTIONS

In extreme crisis (war, planetary event, systemic collapse):

Temporary reductions may be enacted if:

• 75% Global LOTUS vote
• Automatic expiration after 90 days
• Mandatory renewal vote for extension

Temporary status must be clearly labeled in all ledgers.

---

# V. NON-RETROACTIVITY RULE

Baseline reductions:

• Cannot retroactively justify past deprivation
• Cannot invalidate previous violation records

System accountability remains intact.

---

# VI. REVISION LOCK PERIOD

After any Baseline amendment:

• No further amendment may occur for 12 months
  (except emergency override)

Prevents rapid oscillation.

---

# VII. STRUCTURAL AXIOM

Raising dignity should be easier than lowering it.

Stability depends on asymmetry in favor of protection.
