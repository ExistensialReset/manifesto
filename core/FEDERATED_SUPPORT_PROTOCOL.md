# FEDERATED_SUPPORT_PROTOCOL.md

Status: ACTIVE
Version: 1.1
Layer: Governance / Resilience
Scope: Node Stability & Baseline Continuity
Dependencies: LOTUS_PROTOCOL.md, FLOW_VERIFICATION_PROTOCOL.md, SANCTION_PROTOCOL.md, BASELINE_RESOURCE_METRIC_STANDARD.md

---

## 1. PURPOSE

Ensure that Nodes experiencing structural capacity failure receive federated support without compromising Baseline security or decentralization integrity.

This protocol is restorative — not punitive.
Support must increase long-term autonomy, not dependency.

---

## 2. TRIGGER CONDITIONS

A Node may activate Federated Support when:

- Baseline Capacity Index falls below threshold defined in BASELINE_RESOURCE_METRIC_STANDARD.md
- Infrastructure degradation threatens 6-month resilience
- Structural crisis (climate, logistics, health) disrupts baseline delivery

Trigger Models

- AI-enabled Node: Hybrid (AI advisory + human activation required)
- Non-AI Node: Self-reported request

All AI advisories must be logged and auditable under FLOW_VERIFICATION_PROTOCOL.md.

Triggers are advisory until verified by a distributed human review group (minimum 3 independent Nodes).

No single entity may unilaterally declare a Support State.

---

## 3. TRANSPARENCY REQUIREMENT

Support requires structured disclosure of:

- Resource flow data
- Infrastructure capacity logs
- Inventory & reserve status
- Maintenance records

Data must conform to federation metric standards (see BASELINE_RESOURCE_METRIC_STANDARD.md).

Emergency Exception Clause

If full disclosure is temporarily impossible due to technical or security disruption, provisional support may be granted for up to 30 days while transparency is restored.

No persistent data opacity = no federated support.

---

## 4. ROLE SEPARATION

To prevent centralization drift, the following functions must be structurally separated:

1. Verification Group – validates trigger legitimacy
2. Support Allocation Group – coordinates resource deployment
3. Milestone Review Group – evaluates recovery progress

No group may overlap by more than 50% membership.
All groups must rotate personnel within defined intervals (see LOTUS_PROTOCOL.md).

---

## 5. GRACE PERIOD

- Minimum: 3 months
- Maximum: 12 months
- Must include measurable recovery milestones
- Must include Baseline Capacity restoration targets

Milestones must be:

- Quantifiable
- Publicly logged
- Time-bound

Extension beyond 12 months requires re-verification under Section 2.

Grace is temporary and documented.

---

6. SUPPORT MECHANISMS

Federation may provide:

- Shared production capacity
- Temporary redistribution
- Technical deployment teams
- Infrastructure co-management
- Knowledge transfer

Co-Management Conditions

- Time-bound (maximum 6 months per term)
- Explicit exit criteria defined at activation
- Mandatory autonomy restoration plan
- Rotating co-management personnel

Permanent co-management is prohibited.

Priority: restore autonomous capacity.

---

## 7. FAILURE TO COOPERATE

If a Node:

- Conceals data
- Manipulates metrics
- Refuses recovery cooperation

A mediation step must precede escalation.

If mediation fails, escalation proceeds under SANCTION_PROTOCOL.md.

Sanctions must be proportional and reversible when compliance is restored.

---

## 8. DEPENDENCY PREVENTION

No Node may remain in Federated Support beyond 18 consecutive months without:

- Structural redesign review
- Community consent
- Reclassification of status

Support must never become structural dependency.

---

## 9. PHILOSOPHICAL PRINCIPLE

Federation functions as an ecosystem.

Nodes are restored when possible — not discarded.

Resilience is measured not by punishment, but by the system’s ability to regenerate autonomy.
