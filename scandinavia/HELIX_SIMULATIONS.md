# HELIX_SIMULATIONS.md
## Living System Modeling: How HELIX Functions Across Crisis, Routine, and Joy

**Version:** 1.1  
**Date:** January 28, 2026  
**Purpose:** Demonstrate HELIX operational dynamics through realistic scenarios  
**Method:** Agent-based modeling + ethnographic projection  

---

## METHODOLOGICAL NOTE
These simulations are scenario-based projections informed by HELIX system design, comparable cooperative housing models, disability justice research, and ethnographic studies of intentional communities.

They are **not predictions**, but **stress-tested plausibility models** intended to answer a single question:  
*Does the system hold when real life happens?*

All outcomes are qualitative or aggregated; no individual-level data is assumed to be published.

---

## PREAMBLE: WHY SIMULATIONS MATTER
Abstract models fail. Investors, municipalities, and skeptics need to *see* how HELIX works when reality hits. These simulations answer:

- Does the system hold under stress?
- Where does it bend?
- What emerges that we didn't explicitly design for?

## SIMULATION 1: THE FIRST 90 DAYS (System Initialization)
**Key Event:** Refugium Anima is accessed within the first 24 hours by Isak.

- **Finding:** Immediate validation of crisis infrastructure.
- **Outcome:** Phased occupancy prevents system shock.

**Emergent Conflict:** Sound sensitivity vs creative expression (Tariq / Isak).

## SIMULATION 2: TECHNICAL & PERSONAL CRISES (Month 2–3)

### Crisis A: Infrastructure Failure
**Scenario:** Simultaneous failure of primary and backup pumps during a cold snap.

- **Response:**  
  - IoT alerts notify the Ocean Working Group.  
  - Residents provide manual aeration.  
  - Repair sourced externally within 48 hours.
- **Outcome:**  
  - 7.5% fish loss (within acceptable ecological thresholds).  
  - Community trust (I) increases through shared action.

---

### Crisis B: Human Capacity Collapse
**Scenario:** Lin collapses after overextending beyond baseline capacity.

- **System Response:**  
  - No penalties for missed contributions.  
  - Tasks redistributed without social debt.  
  - Silent companionship and prepared meals provided.
- **Outcome:**  
  - Lin’s baseline protected.  
  - Flow prioritized over performance, validating HELIX core logic.

## SIMULATION 3: WINTER DEPRESSION (Month 6)
**Context:** Gothenburg’s darkness triggers seasonal affective symptoms in ~45% of residents.

- **Emergent Adaptation:**  
  - Residents convert the Refugium Creation Room into a communal Light Therapy Room.
- **Outcome:**  
  - All crises managed internally.  
  - 0 emergency room visits.  
  - Crisis strengthens social cohesion instead of fragmenting it.

## SIMULATION 4: ECONOMIC SHOCK (Month 10)
**Scenario:** Jonas (high contributor) loses his job during a regional recession.

- **Protocol Activated:** Economic Hardship Protocol.
  - Fees suspended.
  - Jonas converts IT skills into maintenance and systems support.
- **Outcome:**  
  - Housing retained.  
  - Eviction risk: 0%.  
  - Trust in community (I) increases from 0.1 → 0.4.

## SIMULATION 5: CASCADING FAILURE (System Stress Test)
**Scenario:**  
- Storm damage to solar panels  
- Pathogen detected in Ocean system  
- 8 residents temporarily unemployed  

**Emergency Response (Governance-Guided):**
1. Energy rationing enacted (L reduced but maintained above dignity baseline).
2. External crowdfunding campaign launched (successful).
3. Refugium usage spikes, preventing mental health cascade.

*Decision-making follows cooperative emergency governance protocols  
(see STRATEGIC_DEFENSE_PRECEDENT_TOOLKIT.md).*

**Conclusion:**  
The 15% reserve fund and Refugium safety valve prevent total system collapse.

- 90% of residents vote to stay post-crisis.
- Remaining 10% relocate with full support.
