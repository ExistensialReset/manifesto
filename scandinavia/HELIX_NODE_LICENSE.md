## üõ°Ô∏è HELIX ADDENDUM (Critical Additions)

Beyond standard CC terms, HELIX includes **architectural and ethical protections** to prevent systemic capture.

---

### ARTICLE 1: THE COOPERATIVE MANDATE

Any physical implementation of HELIX_NODE **must be**:

#### 1.1 Resident-Owned Cooperative
- No landlords extracting rent as profit
- No external investors with equity ownership
- Residents collectively own and govern the building
- Decision-making power rests with those who live there

#### 1.2 Non-Speculative Housing
- Units cannot be sold for individual profit
- No market-rate appreciation mechanisms
- When residents leave, units return to cooperative (buyout at cost)
- Building cannot be used as financial collateral for speculative debt

#### 1.3 Transparent Governance
- All budgets publicly accessible to residents
- Major decisions require resident vote (not management override)
- Conflict resolution processes documented and fair
- No hidden fees or profit extraction disguised as "operational costs"

---

### ARTICLE 2: THE BASELINE PROTECTION

No HELIX implementation may:

#### 2.1 Economic Discrimination
- ‚ùå Require income verification to qualify for residency
- ‚ùå Charge different rates based on ability to pay (solidarity pricing allowed)
- ‚ùå Evict residents for economic hardship
- ‚ùå Deny residency based on credit scores or employment status

#### 2.2 Existential Security
- ‚úÖ Baseline housing cost must cover only actual operations (not profit)
- ‚úÖ No resident loses housing for temporary inability to contribute
- ‚úÖ Mutual aid structures must exist for residents in crisis
- ‚úÖ Soul Dwelling autonomy protected (privacy, personal choices)

---

### ARTICLE 3: THE LIVING SYSTEMS CLAUSE

The biological components of HELIX are **not amenities** ‚Äî they are integral to the architecture.

#### 3.1 Integrated Biology
- The Ocean (aquaponics/aquaculture) must be resident-accessible
- Mycelium insulation must remain living (not "sanitized" for code compliance)
- Vertical gardens/hydroponics integrated into daily life
- Residents participate in care/harvest (not outsourced labor)

#### 3.2 Ecological Respect
- ‚ùå Cannot "optimize" octopi/fish/plants for maximum market yield
- ‚ùå Cannot replace biological systems with purely mechanical ones for "efficiency"
- ‚úÖ Must prioritize species wellbeing and ecological balance
- ‚úÖ Surplus production (food/materials) benefits residents first, market second

#### 3.3 Maintenance Collective
- Biological system care is shared responsibility (not service contract)
- Training/knowledge-sharing mandatory (not expert-dependent)
- Sweat equity model (residents contribute labor, reducing costs)

---

### ARTICLE 4: ANTI-FINANCIALIZATION SAFEGUARDS

HELIX explicitly **prohibits**:

#### 4.1 Investment Extraction
- No external investors receiving dividends/returns from resident payments
- No equity ownership by non-residents seeking profit
- No securitization of resident contributions

#### 4.2 Debt Weaponization
- Building cannot be mortgaged for speculative purposes
- Loans (if any) must be from ethical lenders (credit unions, municipal bonds)
- Debt servicing cannot threaten resident security (foreclosure protection)

#### 4.3 Luxury Tiering
- No "premium" Soul Dwellings priced higher than others
- Accessibility features are **default**, not add-ons
- Differences in units reflect need (accessibility, family size), not wealth

---

- ‚úÖ Integrated biological systems (Ocean/aquaponics/mycelium)
